Prescription Classifier

Fall 2020

NLU Classifier that attempts to determine how many pills are taken a day in number format from plain test doctor instruction. An example: "Take 3 pills twice a day" would be 6 pills a day

  • - Tests Multiple different models with multiple different pre-processing methods
  • - Models used: Multinomial Naive Bayes, Support Vector Machine, Random Forest, Logistic Regression, and Neural Network
  • - Tests a combination of Lemmatization and Removal of stopwords(Preprocessed), with tf-idf and reverse IDF(Reverse)
  • - Reverse IDF is modification of idf which increases the weight of words that appear in multiple documents(prescriptions), this is because the more important words seems to be repeated frequently. It increases the weight by current * (1 + n/N), where n is the amount of occurences of the word and N being amount of documents
  • - The best combination was found to be using a Neural Network with reverse IDF combined with the other preprocessing methods. Resulted in an accuracy of 90% and a Mean Square error of 0.3
  • - Written using Scikit-learn
